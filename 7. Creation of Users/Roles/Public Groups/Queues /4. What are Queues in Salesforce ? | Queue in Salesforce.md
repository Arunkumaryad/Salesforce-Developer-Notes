# What are Queues in Salesforce? | Queue in Salesforce

## Introduction

In Salesforce, **Queues** help organizations manage and distribute work among multiple users. Instead of assigning records directly to a single user, records can be assigned to a Queue where authorized users can access and take ownership of them.

Queues are commonly used for:

- Leads
- Cases
- Custom Objects
- Service Requests
- Support Tickets
- Approval Processes

They ensure that records are not left unattended and can be processed by any available team member.

---

# What is a Queue in Salesforce?

## Salesforce Definition

As displayed on the Queue page:

> Queues allow groups of users to manage a shared workload more effectively. A queue is a location where records can be routed to await processing by a group member. The records remain in the queue until a user accepts them for processing or they are transferred to another queue. You can specify the set of objects that are supported by each queue, as well as the set of users that are allowed to retrieve records from the queue.

---

## Simple Definition

A **Queue** is a holding area where records wait until a user takes ownership of them.

Instead of assigning a record directly to one user:

```text
Lead → Arun
```

You can assign it to:

```text
Lead → Sales Queue
```

Any authorized member of the queue can then pick up and work on the record.

---

# Why Do We Use Queues?

Queues help organizations:

- Distribute workload efficiently.
- Prevent records from being missed.
- Allow teams to manage records collaboratively.
- Automatically route records.
- Improve response times.

This option determines whether queue members receive notifications when records are assigned to the queue.

# Queue vs Public Group

| Queue | Public Group |
|---------|------------|
| Can own records | Cannot own records |
| Used for workload management | Used for sharing |
| Records wait until accepted | No record ownership |
| Used with Leads, Cases, Custom Objects | Used in Sharing Rules |

---

# Queue vs Role

| Queue | Role |
|---------|------|
| Work distribution | Hierarchy structure |
| Multiple users manage records | Reporting relationship |
| Can own records | Cannot own records directly |

# Interview Questions

## What is a Queue in Salesforce?

A Queue is a holding area where records wait until an authorized member takes ownership and processes them.

---

## Can Queues own records?

Yes.

Queues can own:

- Leads
- Cases
- Tasks
- Custom Objects
- Other supported objects

---

## What is the purpose of a Queue?

To distribute records among multiple users and manage workloads efficiently.

---

## Can Roles be added to a Queue?

Yes.

Queues can contain:

- Users
- Roles
- Public Groups

---

## What is a Supported Object?

A Supported Object is an object whose records can be owned by the Queue.

---

## Difference between Queue and Public Group?

Queue is used for record ownership and workload management, while Public Group is used primarily for sharing records.

# Summary

A Queue in Salesforce is a shared work area that allows multiple users to manage records collectively. Records remain in the queue until a member takes ownership or transfers them. Queues improve workload distribution, increase team collaboration, reduce missed records, and are widely used for Leads, Cases, and Custom Objects. By defining Supported Objects and Queue Members, organizations can efficiently manage and process large volumes of records.
