# How To Make Email Template in Salesforce | Dynamic Email Template in Salesforce

## Introduction

Email Templates in Salesforce allow users and administrators to create reusable email content that can be sent repeatedly without rewriting the same message. They help maintain consistency, save time, and support personalization using Merge Fields.

Email Templates are commonly used for:

- Customer communication
- Lead follow-up emails
- Case acknowledgements
- Welcome emails
- Marketing campaigns
- Automated Flow and Process emails

---

# What is an Email Template in Salesforce?

## Definition

An Email Template is a pre-designed email format that contains a subject line, email body, formatting, and optional merge fields that automatically pull data from Salesforce records.

Instead of manually typing the same email repeatedly, users can create a template once and reuse it whenever required.

---

# Benefits of Email Templates

✅ Saves time

✅ Maintains consistent communication

✅ Reduces manual errors

✅ Supports automation

✅ Personalizes emails using Merge Fields

✅ Can be used in Flows, Workflows, Approval Processes, and Apex

---

# Types of Email Templates in Salesforce

Salesforce provides four types of Classic Email Templates:

## 1. Text Template

A plain text email without formatting.

Features:

- Simple text content
- Supports Merge Fields
- Fast and lightweight

Example:

```text
Dear {!Contact.FirstName},

Thank you for contacting us.

Regards,
Customer Support
```

---

## 2. HTML (Using Classic Letterhead)

HTML email template that uses a Classic Letterhead.

### Definition

A Letterhead defines the overall design of the email such as:

- Company Logo
- Header
- Footer
- Background Colors
- Branding

Navigation:

```text
Email Templates
→ Letterhead
→ Documents
```

Best for:

- Corporate emails
- Marketing emails
- Branded communications

---

## 3. Custom HTML (Without Classic Letterhead)

HTML template created manually without using a Letterhead.

Features:

- Full HTML control
- Custom CSS styling
- Flexible design

Best for:

- Advanced layouts
- Custom branding requirements

---

## 4. Visualforce Template

Visualforce-based email template.

Features:

- Dynamic content
- Complex data rendering
- Multiple related records

Best for:

- Advanced business requirements
- Dynamic tables
- Custom formatting

Example:

```html
<messaging:emailTemplate>
</messaging:emailTemplate>
```

---

# What are Dynamic Email Templates?

## Definition

A Dynamic Email Template automatically displays Salesforce record data using Merge Fields.

Instead of sending identical emails to everyone, Salesforce replaces merge fields with actual record values.

Example:

Template:

```text
Dear {!Contact.FirstName},

Thank you for your inquiry.

Regards,
{!User.Username}
```

Output:

```text
Dear Arun Kumar,

Thank you for your inquiry.

Regards,
arunkumar2023712390@agentforce.com
```

---

# What are Merge Fields?

## Definition

Merge Fields are placeholders that Salesforce automatically replaces with actual record data when an email is sent.

Syntax:

```text
{!Object.Field}
```

Examples:

```text
{!Contact.FirstName}
{!Contact.LastName}
{!Account.Name}
{!User.Username}
```

# Dynamic Output Received

Salesforce automatically replaces Merge Fields.

Template:

```text
Dear {!Contact.FirstName}
```

Becomes:

```text
Dear Arun Kumar
```

Template:

```text
{!User.Username}
```

Becomes:

```text
arunkumar2023712390@agentforce.com
```

Final Email:

```text
Dear Arun Kumar,

Thank you for your interest in our products.
Our customer care executive will reach out to you shortly.

Thanks & Regards
arunkumar2023712390@agentforce.com
```

This is called a Dynamic Email Template because the values change automatically based on the selected record.

# Summary

Email Templates in Salesforce provide a reusable way to send standardized and personalized emails. Salesforce offers four template types: Text, HTML with Classic Letterhead, Custom HTML, and Visualforce. Dynamic Email Templates use Merge Fields such as `{!Contact.FirstName}` and `{!User.Username}` to automatically display record-specific information. For branded communications, HTML Email Templates can use Classic Letterheads that contain logos, headers, footers, and company styling, making email communication professional, consistent, and automated.
